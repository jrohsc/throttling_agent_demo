Abstract

AI web agents access Internet resources at a far greater speed, scale, and complexity. Deployed maliciously or erroneously, these agents could overload content providers and potentially crumble critical infrastructure. While existing challenge‚Äìresponse defenses, such as CAPTCHAs, work against simple bots, agents powered by multi-modal large language models can easily bypass defenses due to their ability to solve complex problems and mimic human behavior. In this paper, we propose to incur small cost on agents for resource access to deter adversaries. We formalize web‚Äìagent throttling as issuing challenges to an agent that are (1) computationally asymmetric, (2) scalable, (3) robust to specialized solvers, gig workers, or search, and (4) compatible with any agent. As a first step, we introduce the Reasoning Gates framework, which generates synthetic text‚Äìbased rebuses that require multi‚Äìhop reasoning over world knowledge. Unlike trivial puzzles that either do not scale or are easily solved by specialized solvers, reasoning gates impose a substantial solver cost while remaining cheap to produce and accessible to any AI agent. Our framework achieves computational asymmetry (the response generation cost is 5√ó higher than the challenge generation cost for SOTA models), and legitimate users can bypass the gates through authentication. We implement our protocol for a custom website and validate its efficiency on real‚Äêworld web agents. Finally, we discuss designs to throttle billions of agents and methods to allow benign users to bypass these gates through authentication.
